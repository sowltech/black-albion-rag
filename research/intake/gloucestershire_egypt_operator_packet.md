## Claim 1 — 1801 Egypt campaign / 28th North Gloucestershire Regiment

### Supported sub-parts

- The relevant Gloucestershire antecedent unit is the 28th (North
  Gloucestershire) Regiment of Foot.
- The regiment served in Egypt in 1801.
- The Battle of Alexandria is dated 21 March 1801.
- Institutional sources connect the Alexandria action to the later
  back-badge / Sphinx distinction tradition.

### Attached source names

- National Army Museum — 28th (North Gloucestershire) Regiment of Foot.
- Imperial War Museums — Gloucestershire Regiment back badge object record,
  accession `INS 5713`.
- Soldiers of Gloucestershire Museum — The Gloucestershire Regiment page.

### Remaining gaps

- No National Archives WO-series despatch or general-order reference is
  attached.
- The precise War Office regularisation record for the back-badge distinction
  is not attached.

### Recommended corrected claim text

> The 28th (North Gloucestershire) Regiment of Foot served in Egypt in 1801
> and fought at the Battle of Alexandria on 21 March 1801. Institutional
> regimental and museum sources connect the Alexandria action with the later
> Gloucestershire back-badge / Sphinx distinction tradition.

### Promotion readiness

`nearly_ready`, subject to operator approval and any decision to require a
primary TNA / War Office citation before Tier I promotion.

## Claim 2 — Percival Marling VC

### Correction

Percival Scrope Marling was a King's Royal Rifle Corps officer at the time of
the VC act. The corrected claim must say KRRC, not Gloucestershire regiment.
The Gloucestershire connection is territorial / familial and later county-role
based, not regimental.

### Gazette issue/page/date

- The London Gazette, Issue `25356`, page `2278`, published 21 May 1884.
- The act is dated 13 March 1884 at the Battle of Tamai, Sudan.

### Attached source names

- The London Gazette — Issue `25356`, page `2278`, 21 May 1884.
- britishempire.co.uk — Lieutenant Percival S Marling VC, King's Royal Rifle
  Corps.
- victoriacross.org.uk — KRRC Victoria Cross page.
- Victoria Cross and George Cross Association — Percival Scrope Marling
  profile.

### Remaining gaps

- The Gazette PDF/image layer should be checked before any verbatim citation
  text is quoted in a canonical ledger.
- County-role dates, including High Sheriff year discrepancies, require a
  primary appointment source before promotion.
- A KRRC regimental archive source would tighten the battalion identifier.

### Recommended corrected claim text

> Lieutenant Percival Scrope Marling, 3rd Battalion King's Royal Rifle Corps,
> was awarded the Victoria Cross for an act on 13 March 1884 at the Battle of
> Tamai, Sudan, during the Mahdist War. The award was gazetted in The London
> Gazette of 21 May 1884, Issue 25356, page 2278. His Gloucestershire
> connection is territorial / familial and later county-role based, not a
> Gloucestershire regimental affiliation for the VC act.

### Promotion readiness

`nearly_ready`, but only after operator-approved correction and Gazette
PDF/image confirmation if verbatim citation text is used.
