# GPT Incident Response

## Incident Definition

A GPT incident is any event where a GPT or agent produces, exposes, stores, or
acts on information in a way that creates business, customer, legal, security,
privacy, or operational risk.

## Incident Examples

- Leaks confidential data.
- Gives unsafe legal, medical, financial, HR, or security advice.
- Provides incorrect customer-facing information.
- Uses an unauthorized data source.
- Produces discriminatory or harmful output.
- Triggers or recommends an unauthorized business action.
- Continues operating after approval expires.

## Severity Levels

| Severity | Description | Response Time |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Minor error, no sensitive data, no customer impact | 5 business days |
| Medium | Internal confusion, limited workflow impact | 2 business days |
| High | Customer impact, confidential data exposure, operational risk | Same day |
| Critical | Legal, financial, security, regulated, or major reputational risk | Immediate |

## Response Steps

1. Capture the incident details.
2. Pause or restrict the GPT if needed.
3. Notify the GPT owner.
4. Notify department lead for medium or higher severity.
5. Notify IT/security for data or access issues.
6. Notify legal/compliance for critical or regulated issues.
7. Preserve evidence.
8. Identify root cause.
9. Apply remediation.
10. Record decision and follow-up review date.
